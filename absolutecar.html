About

Absolute Car came about through a project with a peer at AIE. The brief was to create a game in Unity then build it for multiple platforms. We managed to port it to PC, iOS, Android, PS4, PS Vita and NVIDIA SHIELDâ„¢. We were lucky enough to have the opportunity to build to PS4 and PS Vita. I wanted to take advantage of porting to Vita so I came up with the idea of splitscreen on a Vita. This came about with the Nintendo Switch's recent release. I decided to have one player on one side and the other player on the other side with their own camera facing themselves. Similarly to how multiplayer phone games work but on a Vita with the buttons and joystick. In relation to iOS and Android, we created a single player version and added on-screen touch controls.

This game had an enormous amount of charm based on the car's movement. It is very strange but on Android, combined with the music, it becomes more of a "See what you can do with this strange car" kind of game. This worked better than I expected and I ended up playing this game quite a lot on the train just trying to pull off cool tricks with the soundtrack pumping.
